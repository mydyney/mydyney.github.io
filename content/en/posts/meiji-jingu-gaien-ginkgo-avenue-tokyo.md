## ğŸ‚ Why Meiji Jingu Gaien Is Tokyo's #1 Ginkgo Avenue in November

The ginkgo trees here aren't the typical round shape, but are managed in a <strong>pointed, conical form</strong> reaching toward the sky. This creates a more unique and majestic landscape.

About 146 ginkgo trees line both sides of a straight 300-meter road.

By late November, the entire street turns golden, creating the feeling of walking through a "golden tunnel." This iconic autumn scene in Tokyo is famous as a filming location for dramas and movies.

## ğŸ“ Easiest Access: Gaienmae Station vs Aoyama-itchome Station

The fastest and most convenient way is by subway.

<strong>1. Tokyo Metro Ginza Line (G) 'Gaienmae Station'</strong>

<strong>Exit 4a</strong> leads close to the ginkgo avenue entrance (about 5-minute walk). Just walk from the exit and the golden scenery unfolds before you.

<strong>2. Tokyo Metro 'Aoyama-itchome Station'</strong>

This station is served by the <strong>Ginza Line (G), Hanzomon Line (Z), and Oedo Line (E)</strong>.

<strong>Exit 1</strong> leads close to the ginkgo avenue entrance (about 5-minute walk). Similar distance to Gaienmae Station.

## Meiji Jingu Gaien 'Tokyo Christmas Market'

<strong>"Into the Grimm Brothers' Fairy Tales! Gorgeous Fantasy Christmas"</strong>

Themed around Grimm Brothers' fairy tales and European stories, it creates a gorgeous and fantastical festival atmosphere with colorful lights and music.
