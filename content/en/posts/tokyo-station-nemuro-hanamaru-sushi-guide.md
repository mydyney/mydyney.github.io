<p>Nemuro Hanamaru is a conveyor belt sushi chain named after the fishing town of Nemuro in Hokkaido. It's loved by locals and tourists for its fresh, high-quality sushi at reasonable prices. Today we're visiting the KITTE Marunouchi branch, located on the 5th floor of the KITTE building right in front of Tokyo Station.</p>

<h2>ğŸšƒ How to Get There</h2>

<p>KITTE is connected to the <strong>Marunouchi Line Tokyo Station</strong> via an underground passage, making it accessible without going outside. You can also walk from the JR Tokyo Station Marunouchi South Exit in about 1 minute.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/tokyo-station-nemuro-hanamaru-sushi-guide-04.jpg" alt="KITTE underground entrance connected to Tokyo Station">

<p>Other nearby stations within a 10-minute walk include <strong>Nijubashimae</strong>, <strong>Otemachi</strong>, and <strong>Yurakucho</strong>.</p>

<h2>ğŸ« How the Ticket System Works</h2>

<p>Nemuro Hanamaru does not accept online reservations. You have to take a numbered ticket at the store entrance. Here's how it works step by step.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/tokyo-station-nemuro-hanamaru-sushi-guide-05.jpg" alt="Nemuro Hanamaru reservation ticket counter at KITTE">
</figure>

<p><strong>Tip:</strong> Arrive before the 11:00 AM opening for the shortest wait. On the day we visited, a queue had already formed on the 1st floor before opening.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/tokyo-station-nemuro-hanamaru-sushi-guide-06.jpg" alt="Queue line on 1st floor of KITTE before Nemuro Hanamaru opens">
</figure>

<p>The escalators to the 5th floor start operating around 10:55 AM. Once upstairs, the queue moves to the restaurant entrance.</p>

<h3>Miso Soup</h3>

<p>Miso soup is <strong>not free</strong> and not listed on the photo menu. It's posted on the wall as a seasonal item. Nemuro Hanamaru is famous for their crab miso soup, but we ordered the mushroom miso soup instead. The portion is generous, enough to share between two people.</p>

<p>Two of us ate a wide variety of sushi, from the 176-yen egg sushi all the way up to the 550-yen premium tiers. The total came to about <strong>7,000 yen for two people</strong>. Considering the freshness and generous portions, it's outstanding value.</p>

<p>The sushi slices are thick and fresh, a real step above what you'd expect from a conveyor belt place. If you're near Tokyo Station and craving sushi, Nemuro Hanamaru is hard to beat.</p>
