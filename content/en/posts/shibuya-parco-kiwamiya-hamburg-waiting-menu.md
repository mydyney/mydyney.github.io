## 1. Tokyo Station vs Shibuya Parco: Which Location?

There are two locations in Tokyo: Tokyo Station and Shibuya Parco. For food lovers, I recommend **Shibuya Parco** for two reasons:

**1. Better Waiting Environment**: The Chaos Kitchen area in Parco's basement is much more comfortable than Tokyo Station's crowded corridors.

**2. Exclusive Menu**: Only Shibuya offers the **Kobe Beef Hamburg** and premium steak combos.

## 2. Waiting Times & Tips (How to Skip the Line)

As stated on the official website, this restaurant operates on a **"no reservations, first-come-first-served"** basis.

### ‚è∞ Expected Waiting Times

- **Weekday 11:00 (Before Opening)**: 0-15 min wait (high chance of immediate seating)
- **Weekday 12:00-13:30**: 40-70 min wait
- **Weekend 11:00 (Before Opening)**: 20-40 min wait (lines form even for opening rush)
- **Weekend Lunch Peak**: Up to 90+ minutes

### üí° Tips to Reduce Waiting

- **Arrive 30-40 Minutes Before Opening**: Arrive at 11:00 on weekdays, 10:30 on weekends for first seating.
- **Off-Peak Hours**: 3-4 PM is the quietest time after lunch rush.
- **Avoid Late Evening**: After 8:30 PM, they may stop accepting customers due to sold-out items or capacity.

## 4. Enjoying the Teppan Style

This is Kiwamiya's first teppan-style location. With 24 bar-style seats, it's perfect for solo dining.

1. The hamburg arrives in a rare state, lightly seared on the outside.
2. Use **silver chopsticks** to tear off pieces of meat (for raw meat).
3. Place on the hot teppan and flip to cook.
4. Once cooked, use **wooden chopsticks** to pick up and dip in your choice of sauce, then place on freshly cooked rice. (Must separate chopsticks!)

**üçö Rice, soup, salad, and dessert ice cream are all unlimited refills.**

When you need more, don't hesitate to say:
**"ü•ó Gohan/Soup/Salada/Soft Cream Okawari Kudasai"**
(Rice/Soup/Salad/Ice Cream refill please)
