## Why Is February 22 Cat Day?

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/gotokuji-maneki-neko-cat-day-guide-02.jpg" alt="February 22 Cat Day limited desserts featuring cat paw cakes and Nyan Nyan Day goods">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">February 22 Cat Day limited desserts ‚Äî cat paw cakes and Nyan Nyan Day goods</figcaption>
</figure>

<p>February 22 is <strong>Japan's Cat Day (Neko no Hi)</strong>.</p>

<p>The Japanese pronunciation of the number 2 ‚Äî <strong>ni</strong> ‚Äî sounds similar to the Japanese cat meow <strong>nyan</strong>. Since the number 2 appears three times on <strong>February 22, it became "Nyan Nyan Nyan Day."</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>History:</strong> In 1987, the Japan Pet Food Association officially established this day to celebrate the joy of living with cats.</li>
  <li><strong>Atmosphere:</strong> Every February, convenience stores and department stores across Japan release limited-edition cat-themed desserts and goods, creating a festive atmosphere.</li>
</ul>

<p>Cat-related spending has become such a significant economic phenomenon in Japan that it's even called "Nekonomics" (the cat economy effect).</p>

<p>If you're planning a Tokyo trip around this time, there's one place you absolutely cannot miss ‚Äî <strong>Gotokuji Temple, the undisputed number one cat destination in Tokyo and the birthplace of maneki-neko.</strong></p>

## What Is Gotokuji Temple?

  <img src="/images/posts/gotokuji-maneki-neko-cat-day-guide-03.jpg" alt="Gotokuji Temple sanmon gate with Hekiunkan plaque and pine tree-lined path">

<p>The formal name is Daikeisan Gotokuji (Â§ßË∞øÂ±± Ë±™Âæ≥ÂØ∫). It is a Soto Zen Buddhist temple located in Setagaya, Tokyo.</p>

<p>As the family temple of the Ii clan ‚Äî lords of the Hikone domain ‚Äî Gotokuji is recognized for its well-preserved Edo-period daimyo burial grounds.</p>

<p><strong>Timeline of Gotokuji</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>1480: Founded as Kotokuin (ÂºòÂæ≥Èô¢)</li>
  <li>1633: Designated as the Ii family's bodaiji (family temple)</li>

<p><strong>A Peaceful Retreat in the Heart of the City</strong></p>

<p>The towering pine tree-lined path from the entrance to the sanmon gate and the forest where wild birds flock make this the perfect place to escape the city noise and soak in the tranquil temple atmosphere.</p>

## The Legend That Started with One Cat

<p>While returning from a falconry hunt, Lord Ii Naotaka was passing by the temple when a cat beckoned to him.</p>

<p>As soon as he stepped inside the temple grounds, a sudden downpour struck ‚Äî saving him from a lightning bolt.</p>

<p>The grateful lord rebuilt the temple and enshrined that cat as <strong>Shofukuneko (ÊãõÁ¶èÁå´ÂÖê)</strong> ‚Äî the cat that invites good fortune.</p>

<p>This is the <strong>origin of the maneki-neko</strong>.</p>

## Why Doesn't the Gotokuji Cat Hold a Coin?

<p>The maneki-neko we commonly see holds a gold coin (koban), but the Gotokuji maneki-neko does not hold one.</p>

<p>‚úîÔ∏è Only the right paw is raised</p>
<p>‚úîÔ∏è It beckons not wealth, but "good connections" (en)</p>

<p>The teaching behind this is that completing one's fortune ultimately depends on one's own effort.</p>
