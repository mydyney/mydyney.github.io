## FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

<p><strong>Q. How many cherry blossom spots per day is ideal?</strong></p>

<p>2-3 spots. During cherry blossom season, walking takes longer than usual and photos take more time than expected. This itinerary is designed for 2-3 spots per day.</p>

<p><strong>Q. How long does each day's cherry blossom course take?</strong></p>

<p>8-10 hours including travel. DAY 3 (Tsukiji to Roppongi) is the longest, DAY 4 (Shinjuku Gyoen + shopping) the shortest. Cutting night views brings it down to 6-7 hours.</p>

<p><strong>Q. What if it rains?</strong></p>

<p>Move Shinjuku Gyoen (DAY 4) to the rainy day. Visit the greenhouse for tropical plants, and if rain eases, walk with an umbrella â€” rain-soaked cherry blossoms have their own charm. Other indoor options: Shibuya Sky observatory, Roppongi Mori Art Museum, Tokyo Station underground mall.</p>
