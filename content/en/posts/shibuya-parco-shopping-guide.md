<h2>üè¢ Why Shibuya Parco is the #1 Shopping Spot</h2>

Shibuya has many shopping malls like Scramble Square and Hikarie, but **Shibuya Parco** stands out for one reason: **"Trend & Culture"**.
It's not just about selling clothes; it gathers the coolest art, games, and fashion in one building.
If you are looking for "Japan's current trends" rather than luxury department store vibes, this is the place.

<h2>üéÆ 6F: CyberSpace Shibuya (Nintendo & Pokemon)</h2>

This is the main reason many travelers visit Parco!
The 6th floor is entirely dedicated to **Japanese subculture**, branded as **"CyberSpace Shibuya"**.

<h3>1. Pokemon Center Shibuya</h3>
It features a totally different vibe from other Pokemon Centers. The <b>Life-size Mewtwo</b> in the incubation tank at the entrance is breathtaking. It uses black as a theme color, offering cool, streetwear-inspired Pokemon merch.

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/shibuya-parco-shopping-guide-04.jpg" alt="Pokemon Center Shibuya Mewtwo">

<h3>2. Nintendo Tokyo</h3>
Japan's first official Nintendo store. You can buy diverse goods from <b>Mario, Zelda, Splatoon, and Animal Crossing</b>.
*Note: It gets extremely crowded on weekends, so they might distribute numbered tickets.*

<h3>3. Others</h3>

<h2>üìù Summary & Tax-Free Tips</h2>

<ul>
  <li><b>Tax-Free:</b> Most shops offer tax-free shopping. At Parco, some shops process it directly at the register, while others might aggregate it. <b>Look for the Tax-Free sign</b> at each shop. Bring your <b>Passport</b>!</li>
  <li><b>Wi-Free:</b> Free Wi-Fi is available throughout the building.</li>
  <li><b>Location:</b> 5-minute walk from Shibuya Station. On the way to Yoyogi Park.</li>
</ul>
