<h2>üè¢ Why Shibuya Parco is the #1 Shopping Spot</h2>

Shibuya has many shopping malls like Scramble Square and Hikarie, but **Shibuya Parco** stands out for one reason: **"Trend & Culture"**.
It's not just about selling clothes; it gathers the coolest art, games, and fashion in one building.
If you are looking for "Japan's current trends" rather than luxury department store vibes, this is the place.

<hr class="se-hr">

<h2>üõçÔ∏è 1F: The Face of Trends (Human Made, Discord)</h2>

The first floor is home to luxury and high-street brands.
The most popular shop here is undoubtedly **HUMAN MADE**. NIGO's brand is incredibly popular among foreigners, and there's often a queue just to enter.
You can also find **Discord Yohji Yamamoto**, **Gucci**, and **Loewe**.

<h2>üì± 3F: Fashion & Accessories (Casetify Studio)</h2>

The 3rd floor features stylish selected shops.
Don't miss **Casetify Studio**. You can customize your iPhone case on the spot. It's a popular souvenir choice to make a "Tokyo Memory" phone case.
There are also unique Japanese fashion brands like **Onitsuka Tiger**, making it fun to browse.

<h2>üéÆ 6F: "CyberSpace" (Pokemon, Nintendo, Jump Shop)</h2>

This is the main reason many travelers visit Parco!
The 6th floor is entirely dedicated to **Japanese subculture**, branded as **"CyberSpace Shibuya"**.

<h3>1. Pokemon Center Shibuya</h3>
It features a totally different vibe from other Pokemon Centers. The <b>Life-size Mewtwo</b> in the incubation tank at the entrance is breathtaking. It uses black as a theme color, offering cool, streetwear-inspired Pokemon merch.

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/shibuya-parco-shopping-guide-04.jpg" alt="Pokemon Center Shibuya Mewtwo">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">Life-size Mewtwo at Pokemon Center Shibuya</figcaption>
</figure>

<h3>2. Nintendo Tokyo</h3>
Japan's first official Nintendo store. You can buy diverse goods from <b>Mario, Zelda, Splatoon, and Animal Crossing</b>.
*Note: It gets extremely crowded on weekends, so they might distribute numbered tickets.*

<h3>3. Others</h3>
- <b>Jump Shop:</b> One Piece, Jujutsu Kaisen, Slam Dunk goods.
- <b>Capcom Store:</b> Monster Hunter, Resident Evil merch.
- <b>Touken Ranbu Store</b>

<h2>üçΩÔ∏è B1F: Chaos Kitchen (Kiwamiya Hamburg)</h2>

The basement isn't your typical food court. It's called <b>"Chaos Kitchen"</b> because it mixes obscure dive bars, record shops, and trendy restaurants.

The absolute superstar here is <b>"Kiwamiya Hamburg"</b>.
You grill your own Wagyu hamburger steak on a hot stone.

<h2>üåá 10F: Rooftop Park (ComDay Park)</h2>

After shopping, head to the 10th floor.
There is a <b>Rooftop Park</b> that is open for free. You can enjoy a nice view of Shibuya skyscrapers. It's a great hidden spot to rest your legs.

<h2>üìù Summary & Tax-Free Tips</h2>

**1. Tax-Free:**
Most shops offer tax-free shopping. At Parco, some shops process it directly at the register, while others might aggregate it. **Look for the Tax-Free sign** at each shop. Bring your **Passport**!

**2. Wi-Free:**
Free Wi-Fi is available throughout the building.

**3. Location:**
5-minute walk from Shibuya Station. On the way to Yoyogi Park.
