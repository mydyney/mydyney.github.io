### Times to Avoid

- <strong>Golden Week (typically around April 29~May 5)</strong>  
  Extended holidays can quickly drive up flight and accommodation prices with large crowds.

- <strong>Silver Week (mid-to-late September)</strong>  
  Depending on the year, extended holidays can significantly increase accommodation costs.

- <strong>Year-end/New Year (December 31~January 3)</strong>  
  Department stores and restaurants often close, requiring advance business hour checks.

- <strong>Midsummer (July&ndash;August)</strong>  
  Very hot and humid (except during fireworks festival periods).

### Flight Booking Tips for Best Prices

When searching flight apps (Skyscanner, Naver Flights), set your destination to <strong>Tokyo (TYO)</strong>. This lets you <strong>compare Narita (NRT) and Haneda (HND) flights simultaneously</strong>.

- <strong>Price difference:</strong> <strong>Narita flights (mostly LCC)</strong> are typically 50,000-100,000 KRW cheaper than Haneda flights (mostly full-service carriers).
- <strong>Booking timing:</strong> 3-4 months before travel, or <strong>mid-week departures (Tuesday/Wednesday)</strong> tend to be cheapest.

## 7. Tokyo Travel FAQ

<strong>Q. How much cash should I exchange?</strong>  
<strong>A.</strong> While card payments have increased, many long-established restaurants and vending machines still only accept cash. Prepare about <strong>30% of your daily budget</strong> in cash (yen) for peace of mind.

<strong>Q. Do I need a 110V adapter?</strong>  
<strong>A.</strong> Yes, Japan uses 110V. Be sure to bring an adapter from Daiso.

<strong>Q. Are restaurant reservations mandatory?</strong>  
<strong>A.</strong> Omakase and popular sushi restaurants require reservations! However, many regular restaurants like ramen and gyudon accept walk-ins with queuing.
