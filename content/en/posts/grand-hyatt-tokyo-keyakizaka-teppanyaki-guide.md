## 3. Wine Pairing: Expert-Selected 4-Glass Mariage

<p>If you want to maximize the flavors of your teppanyaki meal, the <strong>Keyakizaka wine pairing</strong> is highly recommended. The curated selection of four glasses -- from champagne to white and red -- flows perfectly alongside each course.</p>

<p>The wine list brings a level of sophistication that elevates the entire experience.</p>

<p><strong>&#127863; Wine Pairing List (4 Glasses)</strong></p>

<p><strong>&#9312; Champagne: NV Pol Roger Champagne</strong><br>
Pol Roger (French Champagne) -- a refreshing palate opener to start the meal</p>

<p><strong>&#9313; White: 2021 Armand Heitz, Chardonnay</strong><br>
Burgundy Chardonnay -- perfect harmony with seafood like scallops</p>

<p><strong>&#9314; White: 2023 Famille Hugel, Riesling</strong><br>
Alsace Riesling -- crisp acidity that cleanses the palate beautifully</p>

<p><strong>&#9315; Red: 2021 Haut Medoc Giscours</strong><br>
Bordeaux Haut-Medoc -- the ideal pairing with wagyu, showcasing the best synergy with rich beef flavors</p>

<p><strong>&#128204; Tasting note:</strong> Starting with champagne, moving through a smooth Chardonnay, and finishing with a full-bodied Haut-Medoc red -- the progression is masterfully balanced. Among Tokyo's teppanyaki restaurants, Keyakizaka's wine list stands out for its impeccable balance. If you enjoy wine, the pairing course offers far better value than ordering individual glasses.</p>

<p><strong>&#128176; Actual Payment Guide (Per Person)</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Weekend Lunch A (&#165;8,500) + Wine Pairing (&#165;8,250)</strong> = &#165;16,750 (tax included)</li>

</ul>

## 4. Keyakizaka Weekend Lunch A Course: A Sensory Dining Experience

<p>Once the teppan cooking begins, the chef's dazzling knife work fills the space with tantalizing sizzles and aromas. Here's a vivid account of the <strong>Sunday lunch A course</strong> from our own experience.</p>

<p><strong>1. Sea Bream Meuniere</strong></p>

<p>The first dish was Nagasaki sea bream meuniere. The fish was seared to a perfect golden crisp on the outside while staying moist and tender inside, accompanied by a <strong>Yuko citrus cream sauce</strong>.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Flavor highlight:</strong> The <strong>Nagasaki heritage citrus "Yuko" sauce</strong> -- sweeter and more subtle than yuzu -- creates a wonderful mariage with the delicate sea bream. The refreshing citrus aroma spreading through each bite truly awakened the appetite.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>3. Main: Japanese F1 Beef Steak &amp; Grilled Vegetable Garnish</strong></p>

<p>The highlight of the course -- the beef steak. Even as the standard F1 beef (80g), the balance between marbling and beefy flavor was outstanding.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Flavor highlight:</strong> The seasonal vegetables grilled by the chef paired with <strong>Okinawa Ishigaki Island sea salt</strong> make for a sublime combination. The salt amplifies the natural umami of the beef, and with every bite, the juices burst in your mouth -- nothing short of art.</li>
</ul>

<p>&#128204; If you want richer marbling, adding &#165;1,370 to upgrade to <strong>Kuroge Wagyu sirloin</strong> is a solid choice.</p>
