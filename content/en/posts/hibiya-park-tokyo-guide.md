## 1. Autumn in the Urban Forest: Hibiya Park Information

Hibiya Park, opened in 1903, is **Japan's first Western-style modern park**, a representative Tokyo walking spot where nature, architecture, and culture harmonize. Located right next to Ginza and Tokyo Midtown Hibiya, it's perfectly positioned for a brief rest during your trip.

- **Size:** Approximately 16.2 hectares (about 4 Tokyo Dome sizes)
- **Admission:** Free
- **Opening Hours:** Open 24/7
- **Service Center:** 8:30-17:30 (closed during New Year holidays)

**ğŸš‡ Transportation**

**Hibiya Park Station** is a transportation hub where the **Tokyo Metro Hibiya Line (H)** and **Chiyoda Line (C)** meet.

- **Tokyo Metro Hibiya Station** exits A10/A14: About 2-minute walk (directly connected to park entrance)
- **Tokyo Metro Kasumigaseki Station**: About 3-minute walk
- **JR Yurakucho Station**: About 8-minute walk

**ğŸ“Œ Mate's Tip:** Hibiya Park is within the Tokyo Subway coverage area. Use your **Tokyo Subway Ticket** or **Suica/Pasmo** card!

## 3. Hibiya Park Best 7 Attractions

### 1. Main Fountain & Central Fountain Plaza

A large fountain with approximately 30m diameter, serving as the park's symbolic landmark.

Currently (2025) under renovation, but the combination of night views + Tokyo Midtown Hibiya building is famous as a photo hotspot.

### 2. Kumogata Pond (Cloud-shaped Pond)

A cloud-shaped pond where autumn leaves, cherry blossoms, and greenery reflect throughout the four seasonsâ€”a signature photo zone.

In autumn, the yellow ginkgo and red maple reflections on the water are particularly beautiful.

  <img src="/images/posts/hibiya-park-tokyo-guide-05.jpg" alt="Hibiya Park Kumogata Pond with autumn foliage reflection">

### 3. Hibiya Park Small Music Hallï½œHistorical Site

Japan's early outdoor small music hall completed in 1905, the historical site where Korea's February 8th Independence Declaration took place.

Under renovation, but the atmosphere is fully felt just from the exterior and surrounding stairs.

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/hibiya-park-tokyo-guide-06.jpg" alt="Hibiya Park Small Music Hall construction notice and site fence">

### 4. Hibiya Park Large Open-air Concert Hall (Hibiya Yaon)

Called 'Yaon,' it's the holy land of Japan's rock festivals and band concerts.

If you're lucky, you might catch the sound of open rehearsals.

### 5. Western-style Flower Garden & Rose Garden

Flowers bloom seasonally year-round, with the May-June rose season being a prime photo opportunity.

### 6. Tokyo Metropolitan Park Archive (Former Hibiya Park Office)

A German-style wooden bungalow building currently used as a cafe, rest area, and garden wedding venue.

### 7. Hibiya Matsumotoro Surrounding Garden

A 100-year-old Western-style restaurant that has been with the park since 1903.

The omurice and hamburg steak enjoyed while viewing the park scenery are excellent.

## 4. Hibiya Park Running Course

Hibiya Park is a course loved by everyone from running beginners to Imperial Palace run enthusiasts.

- **Park interior 1 lap:** Approximately 1.5km
- **Imperial Palace outer run:** Approximately 5km

**Recommended Running Flow:**
1. Hibiya Park warm-up 1km
2. Imperial Palace 5km main run
3. Cool-down stretching on Hibiya Park benches
