## <strong>1. Why is Sato Yosuke Special? (Inaniwa Udon)</strong>

It's not just famous because celebrities visit.

Ginza Sato Yosuke is the most authentic keeper of Inaniwa udon culture, one of the pillars of Japanese udon tradition. Knowing the background makes each bite more meaningful.

### <strong>üëë 165 Years of Handcrafted Mastery (ImperialÁåÆ‰∏ä)</strong>

Founded in 1860, Sato Yosuke is a 165-year-old establishment that has beenÁåÆ‰∏ä (presented) to the Imperial Household since the Meiji era, earning recognition for its quality. Unlike machine-made noodles, the artisan process of twisting and stretching the dough by hand creates <strong>microscopic air pockets</strong> within the noodles, giving them an unbreakable resilience and silky texture despite their thinness.

### <strong>üçú One of Japan's Three Major Udon Styles</strong>

Japan has three major udon styles known as Sanuki, Inaniwa, and Mizusawa. Among them, <strong>Inaniwa udon from Akita Prefecture</strong> is flat and thin like knife-cut noodles, yet boasts an incredibly smooth texture.

## <strong>FAQ | Frequently Asked Questions</strong>

<p><strong>Q1. Is there a service charge during dinner?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> Yes, there is. A 10% service charge applies during dinner hours (after 17:00).</p>

<p><strong>Q2. Can I pay with a card?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> Yes, card payments are accepted.</p>

<p><strong>Q3. Will I feel awkward dining alone?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> Not at all! Solo diners are often seated before larger groups.</p>

<p><strong>Q4. Can I bring children or strollers?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> Yes, you can. However, during busy times, you may be asked to fold and store your stroller.</p>
