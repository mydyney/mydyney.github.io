## 1. Where's the Free Observation Deck in Shibuya? The Hikarie 11F Sky Lobby

<p>If you want to enjoy stunning Shibuya night views for free during your Tokyo trip, the smartest and most practical choice is the <strong>Shibuya Hikarie 11F Sky Lobby</strong>.</p>

<p>Among <strong>free observation decks in Tokyo</strong> that require no reservation, this one has the best accessibility. With no waiting time, you can comfortably take in the cityscape at your own pace.</p>

### Is the Shibuya Hikarie Observation Deck Really Free?

<p><strong>Yes!</strong> There's no admission fee and no reservation required ‚Äî anyone can walk in freely.</p>

<p>Unlike the paid Shibuya Sky, the biggest advantage here is that <strong>no timed reservation is needed</strong>. Even if your travel plans change on the fly, you can drop by without any hassle.</p>

## 2. Exact Location & How to Enter the 11F Sky Lobby

<p>Shibuya Hikarie is a mixed-use building with both a shopping mall (ShinQs) and offices, so you need to choose the right elevator bank.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Quick navigation tip!</strong> Instead of using the mall elevators, enter through the 2F connecting walkway and take the <strong>office/observation deck shuttle elevator</strong> on your immediate left.</li>
</ul>

## 4. Hikarie 11F Night Views: Day vs Night Comparison

<p>The <strong>Shibuya Hikarie 11F</strong> Sky Lobby offers excellent accessibility and views in equal measure. The unobstructed sightlines rival many paid observation decks, and the scenery changes completely depending on when you visit.</p>

### Daytime: Crisp Urban Panorama

<p>During the day, the Shibuya skyline and sprawling cityscape unfold before you in sharp detail. The 11th-floor vantage point is the perfect height to feel the energy of the streets below while still getting a sweeping view.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/shibuya-hikarie-11f-sky-lobby-guide-05.jpg" alt="Shibuya Hikarie 11F daytime panoramic city view">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">Hikarie 11F: Daytime Panorama ‚Äî Crisp Urban Skyline View</figcaption>

### Night & Magic Hour: The Real Reason to Visit Hikarie

<p>Many people debate between this spot and the Scramble Square 12F nearby. The verdict: for <strong>sunset and magic hour views, the west-facing Hikarie 11F wins</strong>.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Directional advantage:</strong> Unlike Scramble Square which faces north, <strong>Hikarie 11F faces west</strong> ‚Äî directly toward the setting sun.</li>
  <li><strong>Best timing:</strong> Arrive during <strong>magic hour (20‚Äì30 minutes before sunset)</strong> to catch the sky turning red as it blends with the city's neon lights ‚Äî a truly spectacular sight.</li>
</ul>

<p>Despite being a free space, leaning against the window and watching the crimson sunset will be one of the most moving moments of your Tokyo trip.</p>

  <img src="/images/posts/shibuya-hikarie-11f-sky-lobby-guide-06.jpg" alt="Shibuya Hikarie 11F magic hour sunset and night view">

### Scramble Crossing Photo Spot

<p>A question many visitors have: <em>Can you see the Scramble Crossing from Hikarie?</em> The answer is <strong>YES</strong>.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Where to stand:</strong> Head to the far-right side of the Sky Lobby windows.</li>
  <li><strong>What you'll see:</strong> Crowds of pedestrians crossing in all directions and the endless flow of car headlights creating a mesmerizing scene from above.</li>
  <li><strong>Recommendation:</strong> For dynamic footage, we highly recommend time-lapse recording.</li>
</ul>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/shibuya-hikarie-11f-sky-lobby-guide-07.jpg" alt="Shibuya Scramble Crossing viewed from Hikarie 11F Sky Lobby">

<ul>
  <li><strong>Press lens to glass:</strong> Hold your phone lens flush against the window to block indoor reflections for much sharper photos.</li>

### After the View: Seamless Indoor Dining

<ul>

  <li><strong>Weather-proof route:</strong> <strong>Night views ‚Üí dinner ‚Üí station</strong> ‚Äî the entire journey stays indoors, so you can wrap up your evening regardless of weather.</li>

## 5. Hikarie 11F Coin Lockers: Complete Guide

<p>The coin lockers on Hikarie's 11th floor are a lifesaver for travelers carrying heavy bags. Instead of lugging shopping bags to the observation deck, stash your stuff and enjoy the view in comfort.</p>

### 11F Locker Details

<ul>
  <li><strong>Location:</strong> Near the 11F lobby elevators (paid service)</li>
  <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 07:00 ‚Äì 24:00</li>
</ul>
<p><em>‚Äª An additional day's fee will be charged after 24:00, so watch the time!</em></p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Payment methods:</strong> Cash, <strong>credit cards, IC transit cards (Suica, PASMO), and PayPay</strong> ‚Äî convenient for international visitors too.</li>
</ul>

<div class="tip-box">
<p><strong>Tip:</strong> The 11F lockers are limited in number. During peak times (around 30 minutes before sunset), they can fill up ‚Äî so stash your bags early.</p>
