## ‚ú® Tokyo Locals' #1 Neighborhood: Kichijoji

Kichijoji offers a completely different charm from Shinjuku or Shibuya. With the vast Inokashira Park's nature harmoniously blending with unique shopping streets and restaurants, it's consistently ranked as Tokyo locals' "most desired place to live." If you want to enjoy leisure, romance, shopping, and gastronomy all in one place, this is your best choice.

## üö∂‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Perfect Half-Day Kichijoji Walking Course

**üìå Travel Tip: The 'Shinjuku-Kichijoji-Shibuya' Triangle Course**

Kichijoji is perfectly positioned on both the **JR Chuo Line (connecting Shinjuku)** and **Keio Inokashira Line (connecting Shibuya)**, making it ideal for a "triangle course" visiting all three areas in one day.

- **Recommended Route:** Start from Shinjuku in the morning, enjoy Kichijoji's leisure, then head to Shibuya in the afternoon for shopping and vibrant nightlife. (The reverse route starting from Shibuya works great too!)

**Walking Course:**

1. **Start at Kichijoji Station** ‚Üí Exit from the south exit (park exit) and head to **Inokashira Park** for a stroll
2. (Optional) Walk toward **Ghibli Museum** at the park's western end, enjoying the forest path
3. Try the famous **Satou Menchikatsu** at Sun Road shopping street
4. Freely explore hidden accessory and vintage shops in the surrounding alleys
5. Check out **Harmonica Yokocho** alley at the north exit
