## üöá How to Get to the Roppongi Hills Observatory (Location & Entrance)

<p>The Roppongi Hills Observatory is on the 52nd floor of Mori Tower.</p>
<p>Go through Museum Cone on the 2nd floor to the 3rd floor entrance.</p>

<p><strong>1. Take the escalator out of Roppongi Station Exit 1C on the Hibiya Line.</strong></p>

## üëÄ Stunning Views Through the Windows ‚Äî A Complete Guide by Direction

<p>The Roppongi Hills Observatory "Tokyo City View" sits at 250 meters above sea level, with floor-to-ceiling glass walls reaching 11 meters high, offering an overwhelming sense of openness.</p>

<p>From here, you can enjoy a 360-degree panoramic view of Tokyo's major landmarks.</p>
<p>Here's a complete guide to the scenery from each direction!</p>

<p><strong>‚ë† East View: The Heart of Tokyo ‚Äî Tokyo Tower</strong></p>

<p>Without a doubt, this is the highlight of the observatory.</p>
<p>You can see Tokyo Tower from closer than any other observation deck, its presence overwhelming and radiant.</p>
<p>During the day, you'll see the vivid red steel tower in all its glory; after sunset, it sparkles like a jewel right before your eyes. Behind it, the Rainbow Bridge of Odaiba is also visible, making this the most popular photo spot where visitors linger the longest.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/roppongi-hills-observatory-guide-10.jpg" alt="Tokyo Tower night view from Roppongi Hills Observatory">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">The highlight of Roppongi Hills Observatory ‚Äî Tokyo Tower night view</figcaption>
</figure>

<p><strong>‚ë° Southwest View: Mt. Fuji's Silhouette at Sunset</strong></p>

<p>This is a special gift that only clear weather days can offer.</p>
<p>Looking westward over the city skyline, you can see the magnificent silhouette of Mt. Fuji rising beyond the horizon.</p>
<p>The chances are highest during autumn and winter when the air is crisp, and the sight of Mt. Fuji set against a crimson sunset delivers an unforgettable experience.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/roppongi-hills-observatory-guide-11.jpg" alt="Mt. Fuji silhouette visible from Roppongi Hills Observatory on clear days">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">Mt. Fuji silhouette visible from Roppongi Hills Observatory on clear days</figcaption>
</figure>

<p><strong>‚ë¢ Northwest View: Shinjuku Skyscrapers and Green Spaces</strong></p>

<p>On the opposite side from Tokyo Tower, you'll see Shinjuku's high-rise buildings forming a dramatic skyline.</p>
<p>It's fun to spot the sharp Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building and the cocoon-shaped NTT Docomo Yoyogi Building.</p>
<p>In front of the skyscraper forest, Yoyogi Park and the new National Stadium spread out with lush greenery, creating a beautiful contrast between urban architecture and nature.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/roppongi-hills-observatory-guide-12.jpg" alt="Shinjuku skyline viewed from Roppongi Hills Observatory">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">Shinjuku skyscraper skyline from the Roppongi Hills Observatory</figcaption>
</figure>

<p><strong>‚ë£ Northeast View: Tokyo Station and Skytree</strong></p>

<p>This direction overlooks Tokyo's central business district ‚Äî Tokyo Station and the Marunouchi area.</p>
<p>Between the dense buildings in the distance, you can spot another iconic landmark ‚Äî the Tokyo Skytree rising into the sky.</p>
<p>Look for the Skytree with its unique charm, offering a different appeal from Tokyo Tower.</p>

<figure>
