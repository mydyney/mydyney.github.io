<blockquote>"The first place every Tokyo visitor should try"</blockquote>

<p>Hidden beneath Tokyo Station lies a ramen paradise. In June 2009, popular ramen shops gathered on the B1 floor of Tokyo Station to create 'Tokyo Ramen Street.' Starting with 4 shops, it has now expanded to 8 establishments. This ramen mecca brings together regional and style-specific representative ramen shops all in one place.</p>

<h3>1. Ramen Gyoku - Niboshi (Dried Sardine) Ramen</h3>

<p>Originally from Kanagawa Prefecture, this shop is famous for its rich chicken broth enhanced with the umami of dried sardines.</p>

<div class="shop-info">
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Opening Hours:</strong> 8:30 AM ~ 11:00 PM (L.O. 10:30 PM)</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open Daily</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.7 (1,098 reviews)</li>
    <li><strong>Location:</strong> <a href="https://maps.app.goo.gl/dVPwKgN9DkeLA1Cs7" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">View on Map</a></li>
  </ul>
</div>

<figure>

  <figcaption>Ramen Gyoku Shop</figcaption>

<p>Their signature dish is the "Special Torori Soba" featuring a clear, clean broth.</p>

<h3>2. Soranoiro - Shoyu Ramen</h3>

<p>Soranoiro started in Tokyo's Kojimachi district and is known for their signature Soranoiro Special Shoyu Ramen using Jidori chicken. Shoyu ramen, based on soy sauce, is the most traditional and popular ramen style.</p>

<div class="shop-info">
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Special Options:</strong> Gluten-free ramen and vegan ramen available</li>
    <li><strong>Opening Hours:</strong> 9:00 AM ~ 11:00 PM (L.O. 10:30 PM)</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open Daily</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.9 (2,140 reviews)</li>
    <li><strong>Location:</strong> <a href="https://maps.app.goo.gl/PbQQiBZtpTZFsykT6" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">View on Map</a></li>
  </ul>

<h3>3. Rokurinsha - Tsukemen</h3>

<p>Rokurinsha is the shop that sparked the global tsukemen craze, with their Special Tsukemen as the signature dish.</p>

<div class="shop-info">
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Opening Hours:</strong> 7:00 AM ~ 11:00 PM (L.O. 10:30 PM)</li>
    <li><strong>Morning Menu:</strong> Only tsukemen varieties available until 10:00 AM</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open Daily</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 4.1 (4,527 reviews)</li>
    <li><strong>Location:</strong> <a href="https://maps.app.goo.gl/dPDVZCyohCFoUkmK6" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">View on Map</a></li>
  </ul>
</div>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-7.png" alt="Rokurinsha Special Tsukemen">
  <figcaption>Rokurinsha Special Tsukemen</figcaption>
</figure>

<p>Tsukemen is a style where thick noodles are dipped into concentrated broth made from pork, chicken, and vegetables simmered for hours. The perfect harmony of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors creates an exceptional taste. For those who enjoy spicier flavors, add the chili seasoning available on the table.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-8.png" alt="How to eat tsukemen style ramen">
  <figcaption>Tsukemen Dining Style</figcaption>
</figure>

<p>There's also a branch on the 6th floor of Tokyo Skytree Solamachi.</p>

<h3>4. Hirugao - Shio Ramen</h3>

<p>Hirugao is a brand under Ramen Setagaya, specializing in seafood-based broth featuring scallops and sea scallops, paired with aromatic noodles made from Hokkaido wheat. Their signature dish is Shio Ramen Hirugao. Shio ramen uses salt as its base, emphasizing the pure, natural flavors of the broth.</p>

<div class="shop-info">

<h3>5. Oreshiki Jun - Tonkotsu Ramen</h3>

<p>Oreshiki Jun is another Ramen Setagaya brand, with their signature Teriyaki Chashu Ramen. Tonkotsu ramen originated in Kyushu region, featuring rich and creamy broth made from fresh pork bones simmered for over 24 hours. The ramen offers a rich and smooth flavor profile.</p>

<h3>6. Hanamichian - Miso Ramen</h3>

<p>Hanamichian is a miso ramen specialist that was selected for the Michelin Bib Gourmand for two consecutive years (2015-2016). Their signature dish is the Special Miso Soup Ramen. Miso ramen originated in Sapporo, Hokkaido, and is characterized by its miso (soybean paste) based broth.</p>

<h3>7. Gakushinya - Iekei Ramen</h3>

<p>Gakushinya breaks conventional boundaries with their smooth broth, special noodles, and chashu made from three different cuts of pork for the ultimate taste experience. Their signature dish is the Gakushinya Special Ramen. Iekei ramen originated in Yokohama and is characterized by its rich broth combining tonkotsu and shoyu bases with thick noodles.</p>
