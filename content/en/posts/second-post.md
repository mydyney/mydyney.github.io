<h1>Tokyo Station Ramen Guide: The 8 Best Shops on Ramen Street</h1>

<blockquote>"Ramen you won't get tired of, even if you eat it for a week."</blockquote>
<blockquote>"The first place you'll want to taste in Tokyo."</blockquote>

<p>In June 2009, 'Tokyo Ramen Street' opened on the B1 floor of Tokyo Station, bringing together popular ramen shops from all over Japan. You can experience a wide variety of ramen genres, from each shop's signature bowl to exclusive Tokyo Station specials. Here is a complete guide to the eight shops that now make up this ramen paradise.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-intro.jpg" alt="Entrance of Tokyo Ramen Street">
</figure>

<h2>How to Get to Tokyo Ramen Street</h2>
<p>Tokyo Ramen Street is located on the B1 floor of Tokyo Station, near the Yaesu Underground Central Exit. While the station can seem complex, just find 'Character Street' and walk to the opposite end; you can't miss it.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-access.jpg" alt="Guide map for the B1 floor of Tokyo Station">
</figure>

<p>The B1 floor has a simple layout with shops lining a long corridor. Ramen Street is marked in orange on the map, located opposite the blue-marked Character Street.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-map.jpg" alt="Official map of the B1 floor of Tokyo Station">
  <figcaption>Image Source: https://www.tokyoeki-1bangai.co.jp/street/ramen/en/</figcaption>
</figure>

<h2>Guide to the 8 Best Ramen Shops</h2>
<p>What started with four shops has now grown into a major destination featuring eight distinct and acclaimed ramen restaurants. Find the perfect bowl that suits your taste.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-shop-map.jpg" alt="Map of the 8 ramen shops on Tokyo Ramen Street">
  <figcaption>Image Source: https://www.tokyoeki-1bangai.co.jp/street/ramen/en/</figcaption>
</figure>

<h3>1. Ramen Gyoku (Niboshi Ramen)</h3>
<p>Originating from Kanagawa, this shop is famous for its rich chicken broth beautifully blended with the umami of niboshi (dried sardines). The signature dish is the 'Special Torori Soba'.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 08:30 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30)</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.7 (1,098)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-gyoku.jpg" alt="The Special Torori Soba from Ramen Gyoku">
</figure>

<h3>2. Soranoiro (Shoyu Ramen)</h3>
<p>From Kojimachi, Tokyo, Soranoiro's signature is the 'Special Shoyu Ramen,' made with a premium Jidori chicken base. They also cater to modern dietary needs with gluten-free and vegan options.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 09:00 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30)</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.9 (2,140)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-soranoiro.jpg" alt="The Special Shoyu Ramen from Soranoiro">
</figure>

<h3>3. Rokurinsha (Tsukemen)</h3>
<p>This is the shop that ignited the global tsukemen (dipping ramen) craze. Thick noodles are dipped into an intense broth made from pork, chicken, and vegetables, creating a perfect harmony of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 07:00 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30) *Serves breakfast menu only until 10:00</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 4.1 (4,527)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-rokurinsha.jpg" alt="The Special Tsukemen from Rokurinsha">
</figure>
<p>Even at 2 PM on a weekday, the wait was about 30 minutes. Payment is cash-only via a ticket machine. The price varies by noodle portion size; the regular Special Tsukemen is Â¥1,150. Add a dash of the chili spice from the table for an extra kick.</p>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-rokurinsha-queue.jpg" alt="The long queue and ticket machine at Rokurinsha">
</figure>

<h3>4. Hirugao (Shio Ramen)</h3>
<p>A brand from 'Ramen Setagaya,' Hirugao specializes in shio (salt-based) ramen. Its clean, seafood-based broth, featuring scallops and adductor muscles, is its main attraction. The signature dish is the 'Shio Ramen Hirugao'.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 10:00 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30)</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.5 (996)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-hirugao.jpg" alt="The Shio Ramen Hirugao from Hirugao">
</figure>

<h3>5. Oreshiki-jun (Tonkotsu Ramen)</h3>
<p>This shop features a rich and creamy tonkotsu (pork bone) broth, simmered for over 24 hours. The signature 'Teriyaki Chashu-men' is known for its deep, satisfying flavor.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 08:00 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30) *Serves breakfast menu only until 10:30</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.2 (902)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-oreshiki.jpg" alt="The Teriyaki Chashu-men from Oreshiki-jun">
</figure>

<h3>6. Hanamichian (Miso Ramen)</h3>
<p>A miso ramen specialist that was awarded the Michelin Bib Gourmand for two consecutive years. The 'Special Miso Ramen,' with its Sapporo-style miso base, is a must-try.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 09:30 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30) *Serves breakfast menu only until 10:30</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.3 (279)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-hanamichian.jpg" alt="The Special Miso Ramen from Hanamichian">
</figure>

<h3>7. Gakushinya (Iekei Ramen)</h3>
<p>Serving Yokohama-style Iekei ramen, which features a thick, potent broth blending tonkotsu and shoyu. The 'Gakushinya Special Ramen,' topped with three different cuts of chashu, is the star.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 10:00 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30)</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.4 (291)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-gakushinya.jpg" alt="The Special Ramen from Gakushinya">
</figure>

<h3>8. Ikaruga (Seafood Tonkotsu Ramen)</h3>
<p>This acclaimed shop, once ranked 3rd nationwide on a TV special, is known for its perfectly balanced pork bone and seafood broth. The signature dish is the 'Gyokai Tonkotsu Noukou Tokyo Station Ramen'.</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Hours:</strong> 10:00 - 23:00 (Last Order 22:30)</li>
    <li><strong>Closed:</strong> Open year-round</li>
    <li><strong>Google Rating:</strong> 3.5 (1,858)</li>
</ul>
<figure>
  <img src="/images/tokyo-ramen-street-ikaruga.jpg" alt="The Gyokai Tonkotsu Ramen from Ikaruga">
</figure>

<h2>Epilogue</h2>
<blockquote>Taste Tokyo in a single bowl of ramen.</blockquote>
<p>Tokyo Ramen Street is a festival of flavors and personalities. Lines can be long, so we recommend visiting early or on a weekday afternoon. Also, most shops are cash-only, so be sure to bring yen!</p>
