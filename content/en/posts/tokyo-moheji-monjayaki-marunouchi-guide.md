<h2>4. Reservation War, 4 Ways to Win</h2>

<p>Currently, <strong>Marunouchi Moheji</strong> has almost blocked real-time online reservations, and since it focuses on <strong>phone reservations (+81-50-5594-3062)</strong>, the difficulty level is 'highest' for international travelers.</p>

<p>But there is a way! Here are 4 realistic strategies to eat without reservation stress.</p>

<p><strong>1. (Most Recommended) 'Open Run' is the Answer</strong></p>
<ul>
    <li>This is the surest way. Arrive 10 minutes before lunch opens (11:00).</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>2. Use Google Maps 'AutoReserve'</strong></p>
<ul>
    <li>This is a service connected when you press the reserve button on Google Maps.</li>
    <li><strong>Caution:</strong> It is an <strong>AI reservation agency</strong>, not a direct reservation with the restaurant. Confirmation is not immediate upon application, and fees may apply. Use it as a secondary plan if you cannot speak Japanese.</li>
</ul>

<h2>üí° Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)</h2>

<p><strong>Q. Monjayaki visually looks unfamiliar, is there a specific way to eat it?</strong></p>
<p>A. Yes! When the staff cooks it all for you, you can scrape off the crispy burnt parts at the edge little by little with a small dedicated spatula (Hagashi). It goes perfectly with beer. üç∫</p>

<p><strong>Q. Can I go alone?</strong></p>
<p>A. Yes! There are counter seats, so it is good for eating alone at Tokyo Station. However, I recommend lunch rather than dinner peak time.</p>

<p><strong>Q. Does the smell permeate clothes a lot?</strong></p>
<p>A. It can't be helped because it's an iron plate dish, but you can put your outer clothes in the luggage storage under the chair. When you pay and leave, the staff gives you a Febreze shower! ‚ú®</p>

<p><strong>Q. Any other things to enjoy in Shin-Marunouchi Building?</strong></p>
<p>A. Be sure to go out to the 7th floor terrace after your meal. It is a free observation spot where you can see Tokyo Station Marunouchi Square at a glance.</p>
