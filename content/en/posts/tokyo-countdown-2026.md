## 2. [Romantic Style] New Year's Bell Under Tokyo Tower, Zojoji Temple üîî

<b>"If you want to welcome the new year with the solemn sound of temple bells!"</b>

Zojoji Temple, located right next to Tokyo Tower, rings 108 bells at midnight to symbolically cleanse the 108 worldly desires in Buddhism. The moment when the bell's echo and Tokyo Tower's lights blend together creates a uniquely Japanese New Year's atmosphere.

<ul>
<li><b>Main Event:</b> Joya no Kane (New Year's bell ringing) ceremony</li>
<li><b>Atmosphere:</b> Solemn and romantic. The contrast between the traditional temple and modern Tokyo Tower is stunning.</li>
<li><b>Photo Opportunity:</b> Best spot to capture Tokyo Tower and temple together</li>
<li><b>Recommended for:</b> Couples, travelers seeking a calm and meaningful New Year's moment üíë</li>
</ul>

## 3. [Traditional Style] Bustling Festival Market, Asakusa Sensoji ‚õ©Ô∏è

<b>"If you want a lively food fest like in Japanese anime!"</b>

Sensoji, Tokyo's oldest temple, transforms into a massive festival grounds on the night of December 31st.

## üö® [URGENT] December 31st Late-Night Transportation Guide (Subway NOT Running)

This year's December 31st transportation schedule is different from previous years. You could end up stuck on the streets all night if you don't check this!

<b>‚õî Warning: Tokyo Metro, Toei Subway, and private railways do NOT run all night.</b> (Once the last train stops, you can't move until the first train at 5 AM!)

<b>‚úÖ JR is your only lifeline.</b>

<ul>
<li>Only major JR lines run <b>all night (24 hours)</b> from December 31st night to January 1st morning. Trains run less frequently, so plan extra time.</li>
<li>Yamanote Line: 01:00~05:00 (approx. 15-min intervals)</li>
<li>Keihin-Tohoku Line: 01:00~04:00 (approx. 30-min intervals)</li>
<li>Chuo Line (Rapid/Local): 00:30~04:30 (approx. 30~60-min intervals)</li>
</ul>

## üöÉ Must-Read for Shinjuku Accommodation! 'Subway There, JR Back' Route Guide

"Staying in Shinjuku?" Take the fast subway there, and return safely on the non-stop JR. Here are the optimal routes.

### üìç Route A. [Shinjuku All-In] Stay Safe Without Moving!

<ul>
<li><b>Route:</b> (Walk/Train) Arrive at Shinjuku Station ‚Üí Countdown ‚Üí Kabukicho after-party ‚Üí (Walk) Return to hotel</li>
<li><b>Point:</b> Shinjuku has the most JR lines, making it the most accessible. Best neighborhood to wait in 24-hour restaurants until the first train (05:00) without worrying about trains.</li>
</ul>

### üìç Route B. [Romantic] Shinjuku ‚Üî Zojoji

<b>"Oedo Line there for convenience! JR back for safety!"</b>

<ol>

<li><b>[22:30] Arrive at Zojoji:</b> 5-min walk from station. Must get photos with the orange Tokyo Tower before the 11 PM lights-off! üì∏</li>
<li><b>[00:00] Countdown:</b> Welcome the new year with 108 bell rings and tower lighting.</li>
<li><b>[00:40] Return:</b> Walk to JR Hamamatsucho Station ‚Üí Take late-night JR Yamanote Line ‚Üí Return to JR Shinjuku Station.</li>
