## ‚ú® Most Important! Hachigo Strategy (Reservation vs Walk-in)

<p>Hachigo operates in two sessions: <strong>11:00 AM (walk-in)</strong> and <strong>12:00 PM (advance reservation)</strong>. You must choose one method.</p>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/ginza-hachigo-reservation-guide-04.jpg" alt="Ginza Hachigo reservation system notice">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">Ginza Hachigo Reservation System Notice</figcaption>
</figure>

<p><strong>üü† Method 1: Advance Reservation (TableCheck - Most Reliable)</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>How to Reserve:</strong> Every Saturday at 9:00 AM, reservations for the following week open on <strong>TableCheck</strong>. (Reservation fee: <strong>500 yen per person</strong>)</li>
</ul>

<p><a href="https://www.tablecheck.com/en/shops/ginza-hachigou/reserve" target="_blank" rel="noopener" style="color: #667eea; text-decoration: underline;"><strong>‚û°Ô∏è Make a Reservation at Ginza Hachigo via TableCheck</strong></a></p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Pros:</strong> Enjoy your meal comfortably at your designated time without waiting.</li>
  <li><strong>Cons:</strong> Reservation competition is fierce, requiring an "opening rush."</li>
</ul>

<figure>
  <img src="/images/posts/ginza-hachigo-reservation-guide-05.jpg" alt="Reservations only fully booked sign">
  <figcaption style="font-size: 0.7em; text-align: center;">'Reservations only, fully booked today' notice</figcaption>
</figure>

<p><strong>üü† Method 2: Same-Day Walk-in (Requires Luck)</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Method:</strong> From 11:00 AM, same-day walk-in customers are served on a first-come, first-served basis, but seats are very limited and fill up quickly.</li>
  <li><strong>Pros:</strong> The only option if you failed to get a reservation.</li>
  <li><strong>Cons:</strong> Arriving late may mean hours of waiting with no guarantee of entry.</li>
</ul>
