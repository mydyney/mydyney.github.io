## 4. Roppongi Tonkatsu Showdown: Imakatsu vs. Butagumi Shokudo

<p>If you prefer a more classic, meat-forward tonkatsu over Imakatsu's delicate style, <a href="https://maps.app.goo.gl/8CZtZXuKGmuJjeWbA" target="_blank" rel="noopener" style="color: #667eea; text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Butagumi Shokudo (è±šçµ„é£Ÿå ‚)</strong></a> at Roppongi Hills is an excellent alternative.</p>

<p><strong>Imakatsu vs. Butagumi Shokudo at a Glance</strong></p>

<p>Even though both restaurants serve tonkatsu, their approaches are clearly different.</p>

<p><strong>Imakatsu</strong> is all about the ultra-tender <strong>sasami katsu (chicken tenderloin)</strong>. Using finely ground dry breadcrumbs, the coating is thin and feather-light. A consistent presence on the Michelin Bib Gourmand list and Tabelog Top 100, Imakatsu is the go-to for delicate, refined tonkatsu.</p>

<p><strong>Butagumi Shokudo</strong>, on the other hand, wins with its impressively thick <strong>rosu katsu (loin cutlet)</strong>. Coated in fresh, moist breadcrumbs and deep-fried to golden perfection, it delivers a rich, hearty crunch and deep pork flavor. As the second brand of the premium tonkatsu specialist Butagumi, it offers outstanding quality right inside Roppongi Hills.</p>

<p>Choose based on your mood â€” if it's delicate and melt-in-your-mouth you're after, go with Imakatsu. If you want hearty, full-flavored pork, Butagumi Shokudo won't disappoint.</p>
