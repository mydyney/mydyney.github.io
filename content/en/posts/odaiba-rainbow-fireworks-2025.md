## How to Get to Odaiba Fireworks

The easiest way is taking the <strong>Yurikamome (U)</strong> monorail to 'Daiba Station' or 'Odaiba-kaihinkoen Station'.

<strong>üöá Yurikamome Line (U) Daiba Station Exit 2B</strong>

<strong>üöá Yurikamome Line (U) Odaiba-kaihinkoen Station Exit 2A</strong>

<strong>üöá Rinkai Line (R) Tokyo Teleport Station Exit A</strong>

<strong>üìå Tip:</strong> Yurikamome Line (U) and Rinkai Line (R) are <strong>NOT covered by Tokyo Subway Ticket (Metro Pass).</strong> You'll need to tap Suica/Pasmo cards or buy individual tickets, so charge in advance!

## Essential Items & Local Tips

December Odaiba is right by the sea, so the perceived temperature is much lower than central Tokyo.

<strong>üß£ Essential Clothing</strong>
- Long padding or thick coat reaching below knees
- Scarf, gloves, hat and other warm accessories
- Thick socks + sneakers (absolutely no slippers)

<strong>üéí Preparation Checklist</strong>

## Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

<strong>Q1. Exactly how many minutes are the fireworks?</strong>

<strong>Exactly 5 minutes.</strong> But over 1,300 shots are launched non-stop, making it short and intense.

<strong>Q2. Do they proceed even in rain?</strong>

May be canceled in severe weather (strong wind, heavy rain). If weather is uncertain, check the official website on the morning of the event.

‚úÖ [Odaiba Rainbow Fireworks Official Website](https://www.tokyo-odaiba.net/rainbow2025/)

<strong>Q3. Is it okay to bring children?</strong>

Yes, the short duration prevents boredom and crowds are less than summer festivals. However, prepare <strong>warm clothing</strong> thoroughly.

<strong>Q4. Are there luggage storage facilities?</strong>

Coin lockers near stations and shopping malls (DiverCity, Aqua City) fill up quickly. We recommend leaving luggage at your accommodation.
