## 3. 100% Successful Pokemon Park Reservation Methods (Lottery/First-Come)

<p>Pokemon Park Kanto does not allow on-site entry without advance reservation.</p>
<p>The official website has completely different reservation methods depending on the language you access, so it's important to know the right method for you.</p>

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Reservation Methods at a Glance (Japanese vs English)

<p>The English site used by international travelers has different reservation methods from the Japanese site.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Japanese Site:</strong> Advance lottery + first-come reservation combined (Japanese phone SMS verification required)</li>
  <li><strong>International (English) Site: 100% first-come reservation only (no lottery / no SMS verification required)</strong></li>
</ul>

<p><strong>üí° Conclusion:</strong> For most international travelers without a Japanese phone number, <strong>first-come reservation on the English site</strong> is the only reliable method.</p>

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ English Site First-Come Success Strategy

<p>International travelers who cannot apply for the lottery must be ready to "speed-click" at the opening time.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Reservation Opens:</strong> <strong>2 months before visit date, same day at 18:00 (JST)</strong></li>
</ul>
<p><i>‚Äª Example: April 28 tickets open on February 28 at 6:00 PM</i></p>

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A) & Payment Information

<p>Before visiting Pokemon Park Kanto,</p>
<p>here are the most frequently asked questions compiled.</p>

<p><strong>Q1. Is cash payment available at Pokemon Park Kanto?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> No. All shops, restaurants, and goods stores in the park are 100% cashless.</p>
<p>Make sure to prepare a credit card or electronic payment method.</p>
<p><i>International payment credit cards (VISA / Mastercard), transportation IC cards (Suica, PASMO, etc.)</i></p>

<p><strong>Q2. Does Pokemon Park operate even in rain?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> Some areas may be closed. The outdoor experience area <strong>Pokemon Forest</strong> may be closed for safety reasons during rain or strong winds.</p>
<p><i>üëâ In this case, you'll mainly visit Kayatsuri Town.</i></p>

<p><strong>Q3. Are there age restrictions at Pokemon Park?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> Yes. The core experience area Pokemon Forest does not allow entry for children under 5 years old.</p>
<p>Strollers are also restricted in this area.</p>

<p><strong>Q4. Can I purchase tickets on-site on the day?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> It's virtually impossible. Pokemon Park Kanto operates on an advance reservation system, and there are almost no on-site sales during the opening period.</p>
<p><i>üëâ You must secure tickets through first-come reservation, and only those who can verify with a Japanese local phone number can participate in the advance lottery.</i></p>

<p><strong>Q5. Are there additional costs beyond the admission ticket?</strong></p>
<p><strong>A.</strong> Basic experiences are included in the admission ticket, but goods purchases, some experiences, and limited items may incur additional costs.</p>

<p><strong>Q6. Can international travelers apply for the 'advance lottery' on the Japanese site?</strong></p>
<p>A. Theoretically possible, but you'll be blocked at the SMS verification stage without a Japanese local phone number. Therefore, international travelers should aim for the <strong>first-come reservation opening date (2 months before visit, same day at 18:00 JST)</strong> on the English site rather than the lottery system.</p>
